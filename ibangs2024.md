## Social learning facilitates the extinction of conditioned nicotine aversion

<a href="#/cta0">
<img src="./images/p50/correlation_ext_cta_nicotine.png" >
</a>
<p align=left>
<span id="cite"> Han, et al., Sci Report, 2017 </span>
</p>

Note:
With these inbred rats, we wanted to ask another question, which is the relationship between the aversive response and nicotine intake. One obvious hypothesis was that rats with stronger conditioned aversion to nicotine will self-administer less. To measure CTA, we have two groups, one recives nicotine and one recives iv nicotine. We did three days of nicotine becasuse we knew it was sufficient. After day 4, both groups recived saline. The difference in number of rewards between nicotine and saline on day 4 is an index for CFA. I really hated to discard the rats away after only getting four days data, especailly we went through all the trouble in during jugular surgery. So we added five extinction sessions. we use the number of rewards on the last session as an index of extion. So CFA and Extion of CFA are on the y-axis, the x-axis of these plots are number of nicotine infusions. It turns out, there was no correlations between CFA and intake but extinction of CFA is a predictor of nicotine intake. So this suggests, that the way social learning works is by facilite the extinction, or the dissociation between the flavor and the aversive effect of nicotine.

## Is the appetitive cue driving IVSA?

<img src="./images/p50/fig4.saccGluSolo_flavor.png" width=70%>

<p align=left>
<span id="cite"> Chen, et al., Neuropsychopharmacology, 2011 </span>

Note:
Obviously, there are many questions in this somewhat complicated model. There is nicotine, there is flavor cue, there is social cue. They key question is what is driving the self-admnistration behavior. We've done a lot of expeirments to show that the operant licking behavior is driven by nicotine. I don't have time to show you all the data but here are some of the key findings. First of all, is a flavor cue with strong appetitiveness sufficient to drive nicotine intake without social factors.

---
