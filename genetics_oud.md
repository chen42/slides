## Phenotypic variance 

<ul>
    <li > Genetic
        <ul class="fragment"> 
        <li > Additive: as the sum of the effect of each allele at all loci which influence the phenotype
        <li > Non-additive:  interaction between alleles
            <ul> <li > at the same locus (dominance) 
                <li >  at different loci (epistasis)
           </ul>
       </ul>
<li > Environmental 
<ul class="fragment">

<li > Shared, influence is common to members of a family (e.g., socioeconomic status)
<li > Unique, e.g., difference in parental treatment, prenatal environment, life events


</ul>
</ul>

<p id=cite>
Mayhew, et al., Curr Genomics, 2017
</p>

---

## Heritability


<ul>
    <li > The ratio of the genetic variation to the phenotypic variation. 
    <li class="fragment"> Narrow-sense (h2) vs Broad-sense (H2)
        <ul>
        <li> h2: the additive genetic component, the degree to which genes transmitted by parents determine the phenotype of their children.
        <li> H2: the ratio of total genetic variance divided by the total phenotypic variance, including gene x gene interactions and dominant effects
        </ul>
    <li class="fragment"> Can be estimated using trait data alone
        <ul>
       <li>  Twins, adoptions, families, etc. 
       <li>  Estimated in a variety of ways 
       <ul>
        <li> 9 definitions of h2/H2 body mass index from 32 studies </li>
        </ul>
   </ul>
   </ul>

<p id=cite>
Tenesa, et al., Nat Rev Genetics, 2013; Mayhew, et al., Curr Genomics, 2017
</p>

## Oxycodone is highly addictive 

<table width=70%><tr><td>

* Prescription oral oxycodone is a major contributor to the current opioid abuse epidemic.
* Oxycodone has strong addiction liability even when it is delivered orally in controlled-release formulation.

</td></tr></table>

Note:

In both animal and human studies, it has been found that, for abused substances, the route of administration is one of the  determining factors of their abuse liability. Strong euphoria is usually produced by the rapid pharmacokinetics in the brain following intravenous injection or inhalation. 
Pharmaceutical companies have argued that the controlled release of oxycodone delivered orally is very safe and widely promoted its clinical use for pain management, which led to the current opioid epidemic.
