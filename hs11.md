## Flavor cues conditioned to self-administered nicotine is aversive

<a href="#/demo">
<img height:auto width=90% src="images/hs/flavorcue_conditioned_aversion.png">
</a>

<span id="cite"> Chen, et al., Neuropsychopharmacology, 2011 </span>

Note:
One way you can study this is by combining conditioned taste aversion with i.v. self-administraion.
adolescent rats developed conditioned aversion to an appetitive flavor cue (saccharine + grape odor) associated with self-administered i.v. nicotine. In this operant <i>licking</i> model, oral flavor cue and <strong>i.v.</strong> nicotine were delivered simultaneously upon the completion of a fixed-ratio 5 reinforcement schedule.

## Social learning enables nicotine self-administration

### in adolescent <font color="firebrick">Sprague-Dawley </a> rats

<a href="#/solo">
<img height:auto width=100% src="images/hs/socially_acquired_nicotine_ivsa.png">
</a>

<span id="cite"> Chen, et al., Neuropsychopharmacology, 2011 </span>

Note:
However, with the presence of a "demonstrator" rat consuming the same flavor cue, nicotine i.v. self-administration was established.
No water or food deprivation or operant pretraining is needed. Thus the model is appropriate for studying smoking initiation in adolescents.

## Social learning supports nicotine IVSA with an <font color="firebrick"> aversive </font> flavor cue

<table style="border-collapse: collapse; border: none;" >
<tr><td width=15% valign="middle" style="border: none;">Social, Non-learning</td>

<img width=90% src="images/hs/ise_nse_aversivecue.png">

<tr><td width=15% valign="middle" style="border: none;">Social, Learning</td></tr></table>

<span id="cite"> Wang, et al., Psychopharmacology, 2016 </span>

</p>

Note:
To further demonstrate that the appetitiveness of flavor cue is driving nicotine intake in our model, we replace the saccyhrine cue with a quinine cue. The bitterness of quinine is often used to show rats with self-administration history that will resist the punishment in order to obtain drug. Here we paired it as a contingent cue at the begining of nicotine self-administration. We also improved our control by adding a demonstration rat on the left side of the operant chamber.
<b>NSE</b>: neutral social environemnt, i.e., the presence of a companion rat.
<b>ISE</b>: indusive social environment, i.e., the presences of a companion rat who has access to the flavor cue.
<b>AV</b>: audiovisual cue

## Socially acquired nicotine IVSA in adolescent rats

### Summary

<ul>
<li> This model incorporates the aversive and reinforcing properties of nicotine
<li> Operant responding is driven by the reinforcing property of nicotine 
<li> Flavor cue is associated with the aversive properties of nicotine
<li> Social learning facilitates the extinction of conditioned aversion and reverses the hedonic value of nicotine
</ul>
