## Social learning facilitates the extinction of conditioned nicotine aversion

<a href="#/cta0">
<img src="./images/p50/correlation_ext_cta_nicotine.png" >
</a>
<p align=left>
<span id="cite"> Han, et al., Sci Report, 2017 </span>
</p>

Note:
With these inbred rats, we wanted to ask another question, which is the relationship between the aversive response and nicotine intake. One obvious hypothesis was that rats with stronger conditioned aversion to nicotine will self-administer less. To measure CTA, we have two groups, one recives nicotine and one recives iv nicotine. We did three days of nicotine becasuse we knew it was sufficient. After day 4, both groups recived saline. The difference in number of rewards between nicotine and saline on day 4 is an index for CFA. I really hated to discard the rats away after only getting four days data, especailly we went through all the trouble in during jugular surgery. So we added five extinction sessions. we use the number of rewards on the last session as an index of extion. So CFA and Extion of CFA are on the y-axis, the x-axis of these plots are number of nicotine infusions. It turns out, there was no correlations between CFA and intake but extinction of CFA is a predictor of nicotine intake. So this suggests, that the way social learning works is by facilite the extinction, or the dissociation between the flavor and the aversive effect of nicotine.

### Summary 
## Socially acquired nicotine IVSA in adolescent rats
<ul>
<li class="fragment"> This model incorporates the aversive and reinforcing properties of nicotine
</li>
<li class="fragment"> Flavor cues are associated with the aversive property of nicotine 
</li>
<li class="fragment"> Operant responding is driven by the reinforcing property of nicotine </li>
<ul>
	<li class="fragment"> nicotine can be self-administered with an aversive flavor cue </li>
</ul>
<li class="fragment"> Social environment determines the subjective value of nicotine</li>
<li class="fragment"> Social learning facilitates nicotine intake by enhancing the extinction of conditioned aversion</li>
<li class="fragment"> Nicotine IVSA is heritable in this model.</li>
<li class="fragment"> Familiar rats as demonstrator increase learning rate.</li>
<li class="fragment"> Social signals are not needed to maintain nicotine self-administration, but nevertheless, they still promote intake. </li>

</ul>

|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|

<p>

<div id="twocols">
There are a total of 16913 genes symbols common between human and rat. There are 315 genes under nicotine QTL share the symbol with human genes. Chance of human/rat gene involved in smoking: 1772/16913=10%. For 315 genes, we expect to find 32 genes. The actual overlap is 39. Although this is a small enrichment, calculation conducted on other traits shows that this is an under-estimation. This is likely because the nicotine QTL contained many genes. *: reported in <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30584246/">GWAS catalog</a>
